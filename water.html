NextGen Water Standards

• NextGen Standards are for waterlines to be a maximum depth of 60.0" deep. If greater than 60.0", it must be drilled from both sides of the tool.


• NextGen Standard is to be .750" away from part surface or parting line.

• NextGen Standard is to be a minimum of .375" from any pins or other waterlines.

• NextGen Standard is to be a minimum of .5" from any pockets.


• Unless otherwise stated by customer standards, all mechanisms require JIC water fittings.


• It is NextGen standard that any water located on Top Of Mold requires a drain slot.

• Drain slot is to be 0.75in wide and 0.25in deep.


• Core water is to be labeled with numbers. ie. IN 1, OUT 1, IN 2, OUT 2, etc.

• Cavity water is to be labeled with letters. ie. IN A, OUT A, IN B, OUT B, etc.


• All water hookups are preferred to be on Operator Side or Bottom of Mold unless customer standards say otherwise.




How To's


Note: The water lines need to be placed consistently from designer to designer. This makes it seamless for modifications or engineering changes. See below for standardization of water placement.


Step 1

Select Cooling within the mold design tab on the right side of the screen.



Step 2

Select Add Cooling item with Channel within the Cooling menu.



Step 3

Once Add Cooling item with Channel is selected, a pop-up box will appear with three options to choose. Category, Sub-Category and Item. Choose the tool that is desired. For this example, we will chose Water Line.



Step 4

A catalog selection will activate once the Category, Sub-Category and Item have been selected. Select the component needed from catalog list.

Note: These catalog items are engineered to NextGen standard. Any deviation required needs to be review with engineering manager or component developer.



Step 5

After you have selected the desired waterline, the waterline must be placed on a plane. Not a face. However, the Place on Face option must utilized for this. Each Mold-Base has planes provided from the beginning. These planes are to be used for water placement.

Note: Do not place waterlines or any component on faces of blocks or plates. This will cause errors such cyclic references, broken links or components that cannot be updated.



Step 6

Once the placement plane has been selected you will enter a sketcher mode for waterline placement. Waterlines are placed by points. All points must be dimensioned, and the Snap option is not to be used.

All points are to be dimensioned from the main mold USC. Do not use external references such as edges, holes or surfaces.
